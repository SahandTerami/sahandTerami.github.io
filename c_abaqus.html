This course covers Advanced Finite Element Analysis (FEA) using Abaqus, focusing on structural integrity, nonlinear material behavior, and multi-physics simulations to validate industrial designs against real-world mechanical stresses.

🔬 FEA Workflow & Material Nonlinearity:

Transitioning from CAD geometry to high-fidelity numerical models.

  • Simulation Workflow: Mastering the complete CAE pipeline: Geometry simplification, Partitioning for mesh control, and Step/Interaction definitions.
  • Advanced Material Definition: Implementing Elastic-Plastic behavior, Hyperelasticity, and managing Unit Consistency—a critical factor in industrial simulation accuracy.
  • Solvers: Understanding the application of Abaqus/Standard (Implicit) vs. Abaqus/Explicit for static and dynamic events.

🏗️ Structural Analysis & Mesh Optimization:

Core Analysis Suites
  • Static Structural & Stress Concentration
  • Contact Mechanics: Friction & Surface Interactions
  • Linear Buckling: Mode Shape Interpretation
  • Steady-State Thermal & Coupled Stresses
Discretization Strategy
  • Element Selection (Hex vs. Tet)
  • Shell vs. Solid Formulation
  • Mesh Convergence Studies
  • Computational Cost vs. Accuracy Trade-off

⚖️ Nonlinear Convergence & Result Validation:

Ensuring simulation reliability through rigorous debugging and verification.

  • Nonlinear Physics: Managing Geometric, Material, and Contact nonlinearities while debugging Convergence Issues through increment control.
  • Post-Processing Mastery: Interpreting Von Mises and Principal stresses, reaction forces, and distinguishing physical results from numerical artifacts.
  • Verification & Validation (V&V): Cross-checking FEA results with analytical hand calculations and industrial standards to avoid common simulation pitfalls.
