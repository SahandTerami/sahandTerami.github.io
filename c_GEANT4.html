This advanced module focuses on Geant4 (Geometry and Tracking), the premier Monte Carlo simulation toolkit. Built on C++, it enables high-precision transport of particles through matter, serving as a critical tool for CERN, medical physics, and aerospace engineering.

⚛️ Geant4 Framework & Material Modeling:

Establishing the simulation environment and chemical composition of targets.

  • Architecture: Understanding the kernel, action classes, and C++ modular structure of Geant4.
  • Material Definition: Utilizing NIST databases or custom user-defined isotopes and compounds for precise interaction modeling.
  • Installation & Environment: Deployment in Linux (Lubuntu) and managing extensive cross-section libraries.

📐 Geometry Design & Physics Interaction:

Complex Geometries
  • Logical vs. Physical Volumes
  • Boolean Operations (Union/Sub)
  • Replicated & Parameterized Volumes
  • Visualization & Debugging
Physics Processes
  • G4VModularPhysicsList usage
  • Electromagnetic & Hadronic physics
  • Low-energy (Nano-scale) transport
  • Optical Photon tracking

🎯 Radiation Sources & Data Scoring:

  • General Particle Source (GPS): Designing complex radiation beams, energy distributions, and source shapes.
  • Scoring & Mesh: Implementing Cubic and Cylindrical Meshes for 3D dose distribution and flux analysis.
  • Advanced Output: Data extraction using user-action classes and visualization with ROOT for statistical analysis.

🌌 Research & Industrial Impact:

Applicable in Medical Dosimetry (Treatment Planning), Space Radiation Shielding, Muon Tomography, and High-Energy Physics experiments at CERN. This module bridges the gap between theoretical particle physics and practical Monte Carlo optimization.
