B.Sc. Thesis: Autonomous Trajectory Tracking of a Quadrotor via Kalman-based State Estimation

  • Collaborators: Behrooz Rahmani,
  • June 2017 - February 2018

Motivation

Accurate trajectory tracking is critical for quadrotors in tasks like surveillance and navigation. However, their nonlinear dynamics and sensitivity to disturbances make this challenging. This project addresses the need for a practical control solution to ensure safe and reliable path-following..

Results

We implemented four separate PID controllers to control the quadrotor's yaw, pitch, roll, and altitude. We used an IMU to measure the attitude angles and designed a Kalman filter to estimate the altitude in the absence of a dedicated sensor. We tested the system and successfully enabled the quadrotor to follow predefined paths for simple maneuvers under calm conditions without wind.
