This module focuses on Advanced Computer-Aided Design (CAD) using SolidWorks, emphasizing parametric modeling, complex surfacing, and Design for Manufacturing (DFM) principles to transform conceptual ideas into production-ready industrial assets.

📐 Advanced Part Modeling & Organic Surfacing:

Developing robust, parametric geometries with a focus on design intent.

  • Parametric Design: Mastering fully-defined sketches, geometric constraints, and Feature Tree management to ensure Design Intent stability.
  • Hybrid Modeling: Leveraging Surface Modeling (Boundary, Knit, Trim) for organic forms and high-end industrial aesthetics, seamlessly converted into solid geometries.
  • Complex Features: Expert use of Sweep, Loft, and Thin-feature operations for specialized mechanical components.

⚙️ Assembly Systems & Motion Analysis:

Assembly Management
  • Top-Down & Bottom-Up Strategies
  • Standard & Advanced Mates
  • Interference & Collision Detection
  • Sub-assembly Optimization
Kinematics & Validation
  • Motion Study & Range of Motion
  • SimulationXpress Stress Analysis
  • Mass Properties & Center of Gravity
  • Exploded Views for Documentation

🏭 Industrial Documentation & DFM:

Preparing designs for real-world production and assembly lines.

  • Manufacturing Readiness: Specialized modules for Sheet Metal design, Weldments, and Injection Molding basics.
  • Technical Documentation: Generating ISO-standard drawings with GD&T (Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing) and automated Bill of Materials (BOM).
  • Visualization: Applying materials, appearances, and rendering for professional client presentations and design reviews.
