This course, Virtual Reality (VR) & Immersive Systems, explores the synergy between high-fidelity graphics, human-machine interaction (HCI), and real-time kinematic tracking to create presence-driven digital environments.

🕶️ VR Ecosystem & Sensory Integration:

Balancing immersion, presence, and technical constraints like latency.

  • Immersive Realms: Distinguishing between VR, AR, and Mixed Reality (MR) while optimizing for low latency to prevent motion sickness.
  • Hardware Mechanics: Utilizing IMU sensors, optical tracking, and haptic feedback devices to map physical actions into virtual space.
  • User Experience (UX): Studying cognitive load, ergonomics, and Human-Machine Interaction (HCI) in simulated environments.

🎮 VR Kinematics & Real-Time Simulation:

Mathematical Tracking
  • Quaternion-based Rotation
  • Euler Angle Singularity Avoidance
  • Head & Hand Tracking (6-DoF)
  • Real-to-Virtual Mapping
Dynamic Interaction
  • Collision Detection Logic
  • Rigid Body Interaction
  • Real-time Physics constraints
  • Unreal Engine Physics Engine

🧪 Capstone Project: VR Exposure Therapy for Nyctophobia

Developed a clinical-grade VR application using Unreal Engine (C++) to assist individuals with Nyctophobia (fear of darkness). The system utilizes a gradual immersion protocol, procedurally adjusting light intensities and environmental triggers to monitor and alleviate user anxiety in a controlled, virtual setting.
