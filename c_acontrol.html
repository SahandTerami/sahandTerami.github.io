This course, Classical Control Engineering, establishes the fundamental principles of feedback control, focusing on the modeling, analysis, and synthesis of Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) systems in both time and frequency domains.

🏗️ Mathematical Modeling & System Dynamics:

Transforming physical phenomena into rigorous mathematical abstractions.

  • Multi-Domain Modeling: Developing Differential Equations and Transfer Functions for Mechanical (Mass-Spring-Damper), Electrical (RLC), and Electromechanical (DC Motor) systems.
  • Laplace Transformation: Utilizing S-domain analysis to map poles and zeros to system transient behavior.
  • Time-Response Analysis: Optimizing performance metrics: Overshoot, Settling Time, Rise Time, and Steady-State Error.

📉 Stability Criteria & Frequency Response:

Stability Assessment
  • Routh–Hurwitz Criterion
  • Root Locus Mapping
  • Eigenvalue Analysis
Frequency Domain
  • Bode & Nyquist Plots
  • Gain & Phase Margin Tuning
  • Loop Shaping Techniques
  • Disturbance Rejection

🚀 Controller Synthesis & Loop Shaping:

Engineering the closed-loop response for robustness and speed.

  • PID Control: Designing and tuning Proportional, Integral, and Derivative gains for optimal tracking.
  • Lead-Lag Compensation: Implementing Lead, Lag, and Lead-Lag compensators to balance the trade-off between transient speed and steady-state accuracy.
  • Feedback Logic: Analyzing sensitivity to noise and external disturbances in negative feedback architectures.
