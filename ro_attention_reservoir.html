Developing a Learning-Based Control Architecture By Integrating Attention Mechanism and Reservoir Computing

  • Collaborators: Wenbin Wan, Leilei Cui, Francesco Martinuzzi, Joseph Hart, Francesco Sorrentino
  • September 2025 - Present

Motivation

Standard reservoir computers, while effective for modeling and prediction, struggle when used as standalone controllers for closed-loop tracking. They treat all reservoir states equally and are typically trained to minimize prediction error rather than tracking performance, which can lead to irrelevant or destabilizing dynamics and poor control behavior. This limitation is critical for real-time control applications, where accurate and stable tracking is essential. In this research, we focused on development of a method that enable reservoir computers to directly generate reliable control inputs.

Results

To address this limitation, we first integrated a lightweight attention mechanism into the reservoir computer and evaluated it on an observation task. The results demonstrated that the attention-based nonlinear RC outperforms the conventional nonlinear RC in observation accuracy. Building on this, we are now extending the attention-based RC for control applications. The attention mechanism dynamically reweights reservoir states according to their relevance for minimizing tracking error, suppressing destabilizing or irrelevant dynamics and adapting to changing system states. This enables the reservoir computer to function as a pure controller, achieving accurate and stable closed-loop tracking while maintaining computational efficiency suitable for real-time implementation.
