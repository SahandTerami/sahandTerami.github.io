This course, Advanced Mathematics, serves as the mathematical backbone for high-level engineering research, focusing on the existence, stability, and rigorous solution of complex ODE, PDE, and Functional systems.

🖋️ Advanced ODEs & Complex Analysis:

Analyzing system behavior through rigorous existence theorems and complex mappings.

  • Higher-Order Systems: Exploring linear and nonlinear ODEs with variable coefficients, focusing on Existence & Uniqueness theorems and stability analysis.
  • Complex Function Theory: Mastering Analytic Functions and the Residue Theorem for evaluating complex contour integrals and system frequency responses.
  • Advanced Linear Algebra: Utilizing Eigenfunction expansion, Orthogonality, and Matrix Decompositions for Modal Analysis and Control Law synthesis.

🌊 PDE Systems & Boundary Value Problems:

Classical PDE Models
  • Wave Equation (Hyperbolic)
  • Heat Equation (Parabolic)
  • Laplace & Poisson (Elliptic)
  • Dirichlet/Neumann/Robin B.C.s
Solution Techniques
  • Separation of Variables
  • Advanced Fourier & Laplace Transforms
  • Asymptotic Expansions
  • Series Solution Methods

🚀 Functional Optimization & Approximation:

Applying advanced calculus to optimization and nonlinear approximation.

  • Calculus of Variations: Deriving Euler-Lagrange equations for continuous optimization—the theoretical foundation for Optimal Control (MPC) and Analytical Mechanics.
  • Perturbation Methods: Handling nonlinearities through Multiple Scales, Averaging, and Perturbation techniques for systems without exact analytical solutions.
  • Numerical Foundations: Analyzing numerical stability, convergence, and error propagation in advanced ODE/PDE solvers (e.g., Implicit schemes).
