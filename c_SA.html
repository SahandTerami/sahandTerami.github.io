This course explores Simulated Annealing (SA), a powerful metaheuristic inspired by the thermodynamic process of heating and controlled cooling of materials to reach a minimum energy state (the global optimum).

Thermodynamic Foundation & SA Logic:

  • Physical Analogy: Understanding the relationship between thermal equilibrium, crystalline structures, and energy minimization in optimization.
  • Stochastic Search: Mechanisms of the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm for escaping local optima through probabilistic acceptance of "worse" solutions.
  • Cooling Schedules: Strategy and implementation of temperature reduction rates to maintain search stability.

Advanced Implementations & Hybrid Models:

  • Combinatorial Optimization: Solving the Traveling Salesperson Problem (TSP) using specialized neighborhood operators (Swap, Reversion, Insertion).
  • Parallel & Population-based SA: Scaling the algorithm for complex, large-scale problems using multi-neighbor and population-driven approaches.
  • Hybrid Metaheuristics: Integrating Simulated Annealing with Genetic Algorithms (GA) to leverage both global exploration and local refinement.

Practical MATLAB Labs:

• Step-by-step implementation of SA for Permutation-based problems (TSP).
• Coding Multi-point SA and Population-based architectures.
• Developing a Hybrid GA-SA Solver for continuous optimization challenges.
