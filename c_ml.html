This course dives deep into the theoretical foundations of machine learning algorithms, focusing on their mathematical properties and generalization capabilities.

Theoretical Foundations:

  • Statistical Learning Theory: VC dimension, Bias-Variance tradeoff, and Risk Minimization.
  • Kernel Methods: Dual representation and the "Kernel Trick" in Support Vector Machines.
  • Optimization Logic: Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) and Convergence Analysis.
  • Probabilistic Models: Bayesian Inference and Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE).

Research Highlight:

Developed a Regression-based Model to predict dynamic system behaviors, ensuring theoretical bounds on error rates and model stability.
