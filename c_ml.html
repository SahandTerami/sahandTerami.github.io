This course provides a comprehensive roadmap through classical machine learning paradigms, transitioning into the advanced mechanics of Reinforcement Learning (RL) to solve complex, adaptive decision-making problems.

Classical & Structural Learning:

  • Foundational Models: Supervised and Unsupervised Learning, including Concept Learning and Decision Trees (ID3/C4.5).
  • Probabilistic & Instance-based: Bayesian Inference, K-Nearest Neighbors, and Rule-based systems.
  • Ensemble Frameworks: Advanced techniques including Bagging, Boosting (AdaBoost), and Mixtures of Experts.
  • Clustering Analysis: K-Means, K-Medoids, and Hierarchical clustering with distance metric optimization.

Reinforcement Learning (RL):

  • Markovian Frameworks: Markov Decision Processes (MDP), Bellman Equations, and Dynamic Programming.
  • Policy Optimization: Exploration vs. Exploitation strategies (Softmax, Pursuit) and Policy Improvement.
  • Stochastic Algorithms: Monte Carlo methods, Temporal Difference (TD) learning, Q-Learning, and SARSA.
  • Advanced RL: Eligibility Traces, POMDPs, and Expert-based Interactive Learning.

Applied Case Studies:

Implementation of Autonomous Agents for real-world scenarios, including Intelligent Web Crawling, Network Email Routing optimization, and MDP-based Ranking systems using MATLAB/Python.
