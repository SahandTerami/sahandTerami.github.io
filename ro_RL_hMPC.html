Hybrid Model Predictive Control With Reinforcement Learning–Based Uncertainty Adaptation for Autonomous Driving

  • Collaborators: Mohammad Hassan Asemani,
  • February 2026 - Present

Motivation

Ensuring safety in autonomous vehicle control in dynamic and uncertain environments remains a fundamental challenge. These systems must adapt not only to continuous dynamics and discrete decisions but also to unpredictable changes in the environment and varying driving contexts. Existing control approaches often treat both risk and operational modes in a static manner (either safe or unsafe), or fixed mode selection—lacking the ability to adjust decisions continuously or switch intelligently between modes. This can lead to overly conservative behavior, delayed reactions, or suboptimal performance. In this research, we aim to address these challenges by developing a framework for adaptive decision-making that accounts for both safety boundaries and dynamic mode selection in autonomous driving.
